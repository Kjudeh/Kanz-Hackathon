# Target Audience
> Who Naatiq is for, which jobs it serves, and why WhatsApp voice is the only channel that reaches them

## 1. The core insight

Naatiq has two audiences that only exist because of each other. **Workers** who have experience but
no way to present it, and **employers** who need staff but have no way to find or assess those
workers. The CV is the missing artefact between them. Create it, and both sides become visible to
each other.

Everything below follows from one observation: the people most in need of a CV are the people least
able to produce one, because producing one requires literacy, a laptop, typing ability and comfort
with abstract forms — a stack of assumptions the existing tooling never questions.

## 2. Primary audience — the worker

### Defining characteristics

- **Has real, marketable experience**, often a decade or more, that is entirely undocumented.
- **Speaks Arabic dialect fluently; may read and write it poorly or not at all.** Formal written
  Arabic is a second register they were never taught to produce.
- **Owns a smartphone and uses WhatsApp daily** — usually voice notes rather than typing, because
  voice is faster and does not require spelling.
- **Finds work through word of mouth**, informal brokers, or standing outside a shop. Each new job
  restarts their reputation from zero, because nothing is recorded.
- **Has never had a CV** and often assumes CVs are for office workers, not for them.

### Target trades

### Personas

**Um Ahmad, 44 — domestic worker.** Eleven years across four households. Cooks for large family
gatherings, cares for an elderly parent with mobility needs, manages a household budget. Reads
Arabic slowly, does not type. Every new family asks "do you have experience?" and she can only
answer by naming people they might know. *Naatiq gives her a document that says eleven years,
four households, elder care, cooking for twenty — in Arabic and English.*

**Karam, 38 — private driver.** Fifteen years across three companies. Defensive driving, passenger
safety, route planning, vehicle maintenance. Once stopped an attempted car theft on a desert road
and got the family home safely. He has never had a way to tell an employer any of this before the
interview. *Naatiq turns a five-minute voice conversation into a CV that leads with fifteen years
and that story.* — this is the real, validated end-to-end run.

**Mahmoud, 26 — AC technician.** Trained on the job, no certificate. Knows split units, ducted
systems, gas charging, fault diagnosis. Applies to facilities-management companies that require a
CV upload, and is filtered out at the first step — not for lack of skill, but for lack of a file.
*Naatiq produces the file.*

### What they need from the product

Zero installation, zero typing, zero literacy requirement, no fee, dialect tolerance, and a short
interaction that fits in the gaps of a working day. Above all: an output they can *forward* —
a PDF that lands in a WhatsApp thread and can be sent onward to anyone.

## 3. Secondary audience — the employer

Families hiring household staff, small and medium businesses (restaurants, salons, workshops,
retail), contractors and facilities-management firms, logistics and delivery operators, and
recruitment agencies and brokers who currently work from memory and phone contacts.

Their problem is the mirror image: candidates are unsearchable, unverifiable and impossible to
compare. Hiring runs on personal referral, which is slow, small and biased toward whoever is
already connected.

The employer portal answers this with a browsable, filterable set of real profiles — first name,
trade, years of experience, skills, and a downloadable bilingual CV. **Worker phone numbers are
never exposed.** Contact is requested through Naatiq, which keeps the worker in control of when
and to whom their details are released.

## 4. Why WhatsApp voice, and nothing else

- **Ubiquity.** WhatsApp is the default messaging layer across the Arab world and requires nothing
  new to be installed, learned, or paid for.
- **Voice is the native register.** For this audience voice notes are not a fallback; they are the
  normal way to communicate. The product meets an existing habit instead of teaching a new one.
- **It removes literacy from the critical path.** The user never has to read a form or spell a word.
- **The output travels the same way the input arrived.** A PDF in a WhatsApp thread can be forwarded
  to an employer in the same three taps used to forward anything else.

A web form, a mobile app, or an SMS flow each reintroduce at least one of the barriers that keep
this audience out of the labour market's formal front door.

## 5. Geographic focus

Arabic-speaking labour markets with large informal and migrant workforces — the GCC, the Levant
and North Africa. The bilingual output is deliberate: **Arabic** for the worker's own dignity,
comprehension and ability to check that the document is true; **English** because it is the working
language of hiring in much of the Gulf. One document serves both without the worker needing to
speak or read a word of the second language.

## 6. Who Naatiq is *not* for

White-collar and office professionals, graduates and students, and anyone already comfortable with
LinkedIn or a CV builder. These users are already well served, and designing for them would push
the product back toward exactly the form-filling model that excludes the primary audience.

## 7. How success is measured

The headline metric is not conversations or messages. It is **people who have a CV today who did
not have one before** — every profile is a person who is now searchable, assessable and hireable.
The second metric is **placements**: real employment outcomes, recorded honestly in a dedicated
table rather than estimated.
